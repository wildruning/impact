Move 3 — Prompt
Dimension Locking Prompt
[I just had a sales call with a prospect who seemed interested but did not commit.] Write [3] [follow-up emails]. In each version, change only one specific dimension while keeping everything else the same. Version 1: vary [the opening hook]. Version 2: vary [the structure and flow of the argument]. Version 3: vary [the call to action and next step]. Make the differences meaningful, not just different words for the same idea.

Bonus — Prompt
Sub-Agent Prompt
I want three sub-agents to independently analyze the question below. Each sub-agent works in complete isolation and cannot see the others' output. Here is the context all three sub-agents need to review before forming their analysis. [I have attached our last two years of financials, our current client roster with revenue by client, and a market research report on the service area we are considering.] Review all of these thoroughly. If you need additional information beyond what is attached, research it yourself before writing your analysis. The question: [Should we expand into [specific service line]? What does the opportunity look like, what are the risks, and what would you recommend?] Sub-agent 1: You are a growth-focused operator. You think about speed, first-mover advantage, and the cost of waiting too long. Sub-agent 2: You are a financial analyst. You think about unit economics, cash flow impact, and what the numbers need to look like. Sub-agent 3: You are an industry veteran who has watched competitors try this exact move and fail. You think about operational complexity and hidden costs. After all three complete, summarize where they agree and where they disagree.
